NHL Fantasy

Here are some players on the up come:

There are many players you may want to take into consideration, players that you may pick up off of waivers, players you can try to trade for,
who are replacing star, first-line forwards who are injured. The reason you may want these players is because if they were second line center,
and a first line center was injured, they would move up to first line replacing him which would result in more ice-time.

Player #1: Tyler Johnson (C), Tampa Bay Lightning

Recently, NHL star Steven Stamkos, was awkwardly hit into the boards, and obtained a right knee injury. Seeing that he is a first line centerman
with young star Nikita Kucherov, the second line center Tyler Johnson is most likely to move up a line going from second to first.


Player #2: Kevin Hayes (C), New York Rangers

Kevin Hayes went from being third line center, to second line center. With the injury of former Ottawa Senators centerman, Mikka Zibanejad, he was promoted to second line.
Seeing that New York is currently the top scoring team, with the highest differential, he will be a great fantasy player.

Player #3: Mitchell Marner (RW), Toronto Maple Leafs

The young rookie, Mitchell Marner, was moved to first line right wing, accompanied by centerman, Tyler Bozak, and left wing, James
Van Riemsdyk. Marner started off the season being a winger with second overall draft pick, Auston Matthews, but then was moved to first line
with Van Riemsdyk and Bozak where they found a connection. Marner has 18 points in 21 games played. This is amazing considering Toronto isn't
known for being a goal scoring team, and the fact that he is a rookie (not too popular), means that he is not owned by many fantasy players.

Player #4: Antoine Roussel (LW), Dallas Stars

Even though left winger Antoine Roussel is putting up points, avoid picking him up from waivers or drafting him. He is the current NHL leader
for penalties in minutes, with 70. Keep in mind this is in just 25 games!
